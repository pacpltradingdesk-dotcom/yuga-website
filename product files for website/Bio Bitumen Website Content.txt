SECTION 7: THE 4-STAGE BIO-BITUMEN PROCESS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STAGE 1 — RAW MATERIAL PROCUREMENT & PELLETIZATION
Collect agro-waste (rice straw, groundnut shells, cotton stalk, sugarcane bagasse)
from farmers within 50-100 km radius. Process into uniform pellets for pyrolysis.
Capital Expenditure: Rs 15-30 Lakh | Space: 5,000-10,000 sq ft | Manpower: 8-12

STAGE 2 — PYROLYSIS & BIO-OIL EXTRACTION
Heat biomass pellets at 450-550°C in absence of oxygen. Produces bio-oil, bio-char
and combustible gases. Bio-oil yield: 20-25% by weight.
Capital Expenditure: Rs 80 Lakh - Rs 1.5 Crore | Space: 10,000-20,000 sq ft | Manpower: 10-15

STAGE 3 — BIO-OIL REFINING & BLENDING
Refine and upgrade bio-oil through oxidation at 230-250°C. Blend 15-30% bio-oil
with conventional VG-30 petroleum bitumen as per CSIR-CRRI specifications.
Capital Expenditure: Rs 40-80 Lakh | Space: 5,000-8,000 sq ft | Manpower: 5-8

STAGE 4 — TESTING, CERTIFICATION & MARKETING
Quality testing: penetration, softening point, ductility, rheology, rutting,
cracking resistance. NHAI/MoRTH certification. Supply to road contractors,
state PWDs, NHAI projects.
Capital Expenditure: Rs 20-40 Lakh | Network: 2,758 contractors | Manpower: 3-5

SECTION 16: FAQs (For Website FAQ Section)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is Bio-Bitumen?
A: Bio-Bitumen is a road-grade bitumen where 15-30% of conventional petroleum
   bitumen (VG-30) is replaced with bio-oil derived from agro-waste (rice straw,
   groundnut shells, cotton stalk) via pyrolysis at 450-550°C. It meets all
   NHAI/MoRTH specifications and delivers equal or better road performance.

Q: Is bio-bitumen proven technology?
A: Yes. CSIR-CRRI (Central Road Research Institute) has completed 3-year field
   trials on National Highway NH-44 showing 22% less rutting and 18% better
   fatigue life vs conventional VG-30. India commercially launched bio-bitumen
   in January 2026 — the first country in the world to do so.

Q: How much investment do I need to start a bio-bitumen plant?
A: A 10 MT/Day plant requires approx Rs 3 Crore total investment. A 25 MT/Day
   plant requires approx Rs 10 Crore. Investment varies with capacity, land cost,
   and which stages you handle. 25% capital subsidy is available under MNRE.

Q: Can I get bank loans for this project?
A: Yes. CGTMSE provides collateral-free loans up to Rs 5 Crore at ~12% for MSME.
   SBI and other banks offer MSME Term Loans up to Rs 25 Crore. We help you
   prepare bankable DPR and CMA data to maximise approval chances.

Q: Do I need a CSIR-CRRI license to produce bio-bitumen?
A: Yes, you need a technology license from CSIR-CRRI (or its authorized sub-
   licensees) for the KrishiBind technology. PPS Anantams guides you through
   the licensing process. Note: clients must obtain this independently — we
   assist, coordinate, and ensure compliance.

Q: Do you help find buyers after plant setup?
A: Yes. PPS Anantams has a live database of 2,758 road contractors, 994 bitumen
   traders, and direct NHAI/PWD contacts across 18 states. We actively support
   sales for 6 months after commissioning.

Q: How long does it take to set up a plant?
A: A 10-25 MT/Day plant typically takes 12-18 months from DPR to first commercial
   production. Regulatory clearances take 3-6 months. Machinery installation and
   commissioning: 4-6 months. We provide a detailed Gantt Chart at project start.

Q: Can existing bitumen companies add bio-bitumen capability?
A: Absolutely. Existing VG-30 / emulsion plant operators need only add a pyrolysis
   unit (Stage 2) and blending setup (Stage 3). Investment as low as Rs 80 Lakh.
   PPS Anantams calibrates the exact blending ratio for your existing product line.

Q: What states do you operate in?

   Haryana, Jharkhand, Karnataka, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Odisha,

Q: What makes PPS Anantams different from other consultants?
